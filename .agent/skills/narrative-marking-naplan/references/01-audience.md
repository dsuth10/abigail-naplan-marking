# Audience Criterion (0-6 points)

**Skill focus:** The writer's capacity to orient, engage and affect the reader.

---

## Score: 0
**Category:** No evidence
- Symbols or drawings which have the intention of conveying meaning

---

## Score: 1
**Category:** Contains some simple written content
- Basic text with minimal engagement

---

## Score: 2
**Category:** Shows awareness of basic audience expectations
- Uses simple narrative markers
- May include:
  - Simple titles
  - Formulaic story opening: "Long, long ago…", "Once a boy was walking when…"
  - Basic description of people or places

---

## Score: 3
**Category:** An internally consistent story
- Attempts to support the reader by developing a shared understanding of context
- Contains sufficient information for the reader to follow the story fairly easily

---

## Score: 4
**Category:** Supports reader understanding and attempts to engage
- Supports reader understanding
- Attempts to engage the reader

**Narrative devices may include:**
- Fantasy, humour, suspense
- Sub-genre styles (e.g. satire, boys' own, chick lit)
- Intertextual references

**Language choices may:**
- Control writer/reader relationship
- Reveal values and attitudes
- Establish narrator stance
- Subvert expectations
- Evoke an emotional response
- Encourage reflection
- Display irony

---

## Score: 5
**Category:** Deliberate engagement through language and narrative devices
- Supports and engages the reader through deliberate choice of language and use of narrative devices
- Purposeful selection of words and techniques to create effect

---

## Score: 6
**Category:** Caters to anticipated values and affects the reader
- Caters to the anticipated values and expectations of the reader
- Influences or affects the reader through precise and sustained choice of language and use of narrative devices
- Demonstrates sophisticated control of the reader experience
