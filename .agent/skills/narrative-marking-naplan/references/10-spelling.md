# Spelling Criterion (0-6 points)

**Skill focus:** The accuracy of spelling and the difficulty of the words used.

---

## Score: 0
**Category:** No conventional spelling
- Uses letters but no conventional spelling can be discerned
- Possibly a string of initial sounds, but decoding is not possible

---

## Score: 1
**Category:** Few examples of conventional spelling
- Few examples of conventional spelling

---

## Score: 2
**Category:** Most simple, some common words correct
- Correct spelling of most simple words
- Some common words (errors evident in common words)

---

## Score: 3
**Category:** Most simple and common words correct
- Correct spelling of most simple words
- Most common words

---

## Score: 4
**Category:** Simple correct, most common, some difficult
- Correct spelling of simple words
- Most common words
- Some difficult words (errors do not outnumber correct spellings)

---

## Score: 5
**Category:** Simple correct, most common, at least 10 difficult
- Correct spelling of simple words
- Most common words
- At least 10 difficult words (errors do not outnumber correct spellings)

---

## Score: 6
**Category:** All words correct including challenging
- Correct spelling of all words
- At least 10 difficult words
- Some challenging words

**NOTE:** As the work is first draft writing, allowances can be made for very occasional (1 or 2) minor errors, which should be disregarded when assigning this category.

---

## Spelling Complexity Categories

### Simple words
- Short vowel single-syllable words: bad, fit, not
- With consonant digraphs: shop, thin, much, chips
- With consonant blends: drop, clap, grass, bring
- Double final consonants: will, less
- High frequency long vowel single-syllable words: name, park, good, school, feet, food

### Common words
- Single-syllable words with:
  - Harder two consonant blends: crack, square
  - Three consonant blends: stretch, catch, strung
  - Common long vowels: face, sail, eight, mean, nice, fly, coke, use, close, again
- Multisyllabic words with even stress patterns: middle, litter, plastic, between, hospital
- Compound words: downstairs
- Common homophones: there/their, write/right, hear/here, brake/break
- Suffixes that don't change the base word: jumped, sadly, adults, happening
- Common words with silent letters: know, wrong, comb
- Single-syllable words ending in: ould, ey, ough
- Most rule-driven words: drop e, double letter, change y to i (having, spitting, heavier)

### Difficult words
- Uneven stress patterns in multisyllabic words: chocolate, mineral
- Uncommon vowel patterns: drought, hygiene
- Difficult subject-specific content words: obese
- Difficult homophones: practice/practise
- Suffixes where base word changes: generate/generation
- Consonant alternation patterns: confident/confidence
- Many three and four syllable words: invisible, organise, community
- Multisyllabic words ending in: tion, sion, ture, ible/able, ent/ant, ful

### Challenging words
- Unusual consonant patterns: guarantee
- Longer words with unstressed syllables: responsibility
- Vowel alteration patterns: brief to brevity, propose to proposition
- Foreign words
- Suffixes to words ending in e, c or l: physically, changeable, mathematician
